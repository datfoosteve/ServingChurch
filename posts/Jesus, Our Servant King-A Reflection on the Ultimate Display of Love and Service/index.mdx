title: "Jesus, Our Servant King: A Reflection on the Ultimate Display of Love and Service"
date: "2022-10-30"
description: "Jesus' powerful display of love and service, highlighting how his actions show us that he is willing to go above and beyond to care for us."
---

In a world where we often seek power, wealth, and prestige, Jesus Christ stood out as a servant leader. 
He didn't come to be served, but to serve and to give his life as a ransom for many (Mark 10:45). 
He washed his disciples' feet, a task reserved for the lowliest of servants, and exemplified the ultimate act of love and service (John 13:1-17).

Jesus' powerful display of love and service teaches us that he is willing to go above and beyond to care for us. 
He humbled himself to the point of death, even death on a cross (Philippians 2:8), and in doing so, he demonstrated his unwavering love for us. 
He desires to comfort us in our sorrows, heal us in our infirmities, and carry us through the storms of life (Matthew 11:28).

As believers, we are called to follow in Jesus' footsteps and serve others as he served us. 
This means going out of our way to show love and compassion, even when it may be inconvenient or uncomfortable. 
It means putting the needs of others before our own, just as Jesus did for us.

So, let us cast our burdens at Jesus' feet and trust in him. He is our servant king, 
and he desires to care for us in the same way that he cared for his disciples. By allowing him to wash our feet with his love, 
we can experience the peace and comfort that comes from being in right relationship with our Lord and Savior.

"For even the Son of Man came not to be served but to serve, and to give his life as a ransom for many." - Mark 10:45


- Sam Thomas
