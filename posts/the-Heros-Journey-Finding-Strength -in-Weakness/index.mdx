---
title: "The Hero's Journey: Finding Strength in Weakness"
date: "2023-11-01"
description: "Drawing inspiration from Jesus Christ's journey and perseverance during his hardest times, as a reminder to turn to faith during our own struggles"
---


In times of weakness, fatigue, and heavy burden, it can be easy to feel lost and alone. 
However, we can find comfort and inspiration in the story of Jesus Christ and his journey to the cross.

Section 1: The Call to Adventure
In the hero's journey, the call to adventure marks the beginning of the hero's quest. For Jesus, 
this call came in the form of his mission to bring salvation to humanity. He was called to a difficult and painful journey, 
but he accepted it willingly, knowing that it was for the greater good.

"For God so loved the world that he gave his one and only Son, that whoever believes in him shall not perish but have eternal life." - John 3:16


Section 2: The Road of Trials
As the hero progresses on their journey, they will face many trials and obstacles. 
For Jesus, his road of trials included betrayal, persecution, and ultimately, the crucifixion. 
It was during these trials that Jesus' strength and faith were tested, but he never wavered in his mission to save humanity.

"For Christ also suffered once for sins, the righteous for the unrighteous, to bring you to God." - 1 Peter 3:18

Section 3: The Ultimate Sacrifice
In the hero's journey, the ultimate sacrifice marks the climax of the hero's quest. For Jesus, 
this sacrifice was his death on the cross. Despite the pain and suffering that he endured, 
he remained steadfast in his love for humanity and his mission to bring salvation. Through his sacrifice, 
Jesus conquered death and offered eternal life to all who believe in him.

"But God demonstrates his own love for us in this: While we were still sinners, Christ died for us." - Romans 5:8

In moments of weakness and fatigue, 
let us remember the journey of Jesus and the ultimate sacrifice he made for us. 
Through his story, we can find strength, hope, and comfort, 
knowing that he has already overcome the trials and obstacles that we may face.

- Sam Thomas
